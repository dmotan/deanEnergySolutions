Norfolk Southern Railway

Junita Locomotive Shop

Altoona, PA

1.5MW Cogen with HRSG

Client: Norfolk Southern

Completion Date: June 2016 

Dean Energy Role: GC/Mechanical

Project Description 

Installation of a 1560kw, 480v, 60 Hz CAT  engine (Model CG170‐16) with an exhaust  waste heat recovery system in two (2) custom Prichard Brown enclosures – one for the engine  and the other for the HRSG; at Norfolk Southern’s Locomotive Shop in Altoona, PA. The  project was successfully completed on time and on budget; the owner received their utility  incentive rebate check from the local utility (Panelec/First Energy).

The project scope of work included the  installation of a new 38 kV Load Interrupter  Switchgear (bullpen), medium voltage transformer and neutral ground resistors,  underground ductbank for feeders from CHP to the new bullpen, installation of all Automated   Logic controls for the CHP plant including integration into the facility’s BMS, all the  mechanical equipment and associated piping such as BFW, gas service, meters, and air  distribution, installation of a 24” diameter double wall stainless steel exhaust system, the  general contracting scope including the transport, rigging and setting of the equipment,  trenching and excavation, fencing, and all concrete foundation and equipment pad work.

UDR, Inc.

10 Hanover Square, New York, NY

Generator Emissions Upgrade Project

Client: UDR, Inc.

Completion Date: May 2016

Dean Energy Role: Mechanical Contractor

Project Description 

Emissions Upgrade to an existing 2MW Cat Emergency Generator located on the roof of 10 Hanover Square. This project was successfully completed and will be implemented into Con Edison’s Peak Load Management Program.

The projects scope of work included generator preventive maintenance, fuel oil tank cleaning, and removal of the existing generator exhaust system to accommodate the installation and rigging of a new Safety Power ecoCUBETM TierIV Unit, Control System with SCR/DPF/Silencing with Sound Attenuation onto the roof of 10 Hanover.

This installation required custom steel modifications at the roof level to handle the weight of the new emissions equipment, exhaust modifications, along with all required stainless steel piping, electrical power and control work including modifications to the existing automatic transfer switches onsite.

New York Power Authority

Poletti 500 MW Administration Building

Astoria, Queens

HVAC Upgrade

Client: NYPA

Completion Date: August 2016

Dean Energy Role: GC/Mechanical

Project Description 

Turnkey comprehensive HVAC upgrades in New York Power Authority’s 100,000 sq. ft. Administration Building at their 500MW Poletti Plant. The air handlers, VAV, and pneumatic systems were more than 25 years old. These systems were replaced with high efficiency equipment ‐ significantly improving energy usage.

The project scope included an initial survey of the building’s existing conditions to establish its baseline operation, upgraded their 90 ton AHU on the roof with new Daiken condensers and coils including new steel dunnage, the installation of a new 10 ton Carrier AHU servicing the lower floors of the building, the installation of a new 6 ton multi‐split VRV system for their Metering Lab, replaced and upgraded thirty‐six (36) Variable Air Volume (VAV) units with DDC controls, installed two (2) new 5 hp duplex control air compressors with dryer, filter and regulator set, repaired fifteen (15) pneumatic VAVs, along with upgrading the buildings existing pneumatic air distribution system, and provided air balancing

Valhalla Campus Boiler Plant Upgrade Project

Client: Westchester DPW

Completion Date: October 2017

Dean Energy Role: GC/Mechanical

Sage Realty Corporation

Sage Realty Corporation

437 Madison Ave, New York, NY

Voluntary Standby Generator with Tier IV Emissions Package

Client: Sage Realty Corporation

Completion Date: December 2015

Dean Energy Role: Mechanical Contractor

Project Description 

Installation of a new 1MW Cummins Voluntary Standby Generator with a Miratech TierIV emissions system installed within the property’s loading dock on West 50th street. This project was successfully implemented into Con Edison’s Peak Load Management Program in March 2016.

The projects scope of work included a new fuel oil system for voluntary generator including a 2,500 gallon storage tank, duplex pump system, leak detection system, fuel oil filtration system, 275 gallon generator belly tank and fuel oil fill box with alarms. Custom sheet metal was also built onsite including galvanized ductwork (2” double wall with 16ga. exterior & 22ga perforated metal interior), automatic dampers, access doors, architectural louvers, flex connections, combination fire/smoke dampers, fire dampers and sound attenuation. The buildings onsite sprinkler system was modified in order to accommodate the change in space usage, along with the relocation of existing hot water unit heaters.

Fischer Brothers

Fischer Brothers

633 3rd Ave Standby Generator Project

Client: Fischer Brothers

Completion Date: July 2017

Dean Energy Role: GC

Project Description 

The project scope includes the purchase and installation of one (1) 750kW Caterpillar voluntary emergency generator with a 275 gal. belly tank, and custom sound attenuated enclosure set on the buildings’ roof at forty‐one stories high; along with eleven (11) Automatic Transfer Switches (ATS’) tied into the buildings fire, life, and safety systems. The new electrical feeder distribution runs from the service in the sub‐basement, to the new ATS’ with the final connection to the generator on the roof.

The complex rigging operation includes the installation of a twenty‐eight (28) ton derrick connected into the buildings’ roof superstructure and all associated roof modifications and protection.

The project also includes new steel dunnage for the generator and the build‐out of new CMU block rooms for the ATS’.
